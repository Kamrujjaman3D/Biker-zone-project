Frequently Asked Qes

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?
Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.
How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?
Check for cracks, worn-out padding, loose straps, or faded certification labels. If your helmet is over five years old or has been in an accident, replace it for optimal safety and protection while riding.
My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?
Before riding, check tire pressure, brakes, fuel, battery, and fluids. Inspect for rust, leaks, or loose parts. If unsure, have a professional service it to ensure safety and smooth performance on the road.
What rules should I follow when riding my bike?
Follow traffic laws, wear protective gear, obey speed limits, use signals, stay visible, check mirrors, maintain a safe distance, avoid distractions, inspect your bike regularly, and always ride defensively for maximum safety.
